iRisePro · Product & Consent Design

Parental consent — without needing a second email

How a parent consents for a school-age child to take the AACE64 Success Scan, designed for how Indian families actually sign up.

Design summary · pending legal sign-off · updated 24 Jul 2026

The problem we're solving

When a parent enrols a school-age child today, the flow asks the parent to give a different email from the child's, then verify a one-time code sent to it. But most Indian families have one email — the parent's; the child has none. That blocks the normal family at the consent step.

The change (and what the parent actually sees)

For the parent, the flow stays basically the same — actually simpler. We remove the "separate email + one-time code" hoop. The parent just:

  • enters their name and relationship, and
  • ticks an explicit "I am the parent/guardian of [child] and I consent to processing their Success Scan data" box.

The family's single email is fine. No second email, no code to type.

What backs the consent up (invisible to the user)

The parent has already paid (a real card/UPI payment) before the child can start — a genuine adult signal that's far harder to fake than an email code. The consent is linked to that payment as evidence a real adult was involved. Net result: a stronger, more defensible record than the email code it replaces.

Who sees the parental-consent step

The programme stream already tells us who is a child, so most people are routed automatically with no extra question:

StreamWhoConsent
Class 5–7 · Class 8–10MinorParental consent (as above)
NEET · JEE · UG/PG readinessMixed 16–22One "is the student 18+?" question decides parent vs. self
Civil services (IAS/KAS)AdultSelf-consent

The one small addition is a single "is the student 18+?" question, and only on the mixed streams — so adults are never wrongly asked for a guardian's consent, and it appears after payment, never blocking the sale.

Why this is the right balance

  • No conversion cost — checkout is untouched; the consent lives at onboarding as it does today.
  • Fits the market — one family email, one paying parent, no OTP friction.
  • Stronger evidence — a named parent, an explicit consent, and a real payment behind it.
  • Not over-built — we deliberately avoid heavier verification (e.g. DigiLocker) as disproportionate for this stage.

What still needs a decision (legal)

Two rulings are with KMM / counsel before this activates:

  • Is it "verifiable" enough? — does named parent + explicit written consent + backed by their payment meet the DPDPA §9 standard for verifiable parental consent.
  • Scope confirmation — confirming the Success Scan sits within what parental consent can permit for children under §9.

Engineering builds it behind a switch; it turns on only after these are confirmed.
