Why we're doing this

Right now we lose money in three quiet ways:

  • We lose leads. A parent opens the payment screen and closes it — or browses and leaves — and we capture nothing. We can never follow up with someone we never recorded.
  • We follow up too slowly. A hot lead cools within minutes. If we don't call fast, we lose them to a competitor.
  • We can't measure campaigns. We see visits, but not which ad/campaign actually produced paying enrolments — so we can't tell what's worth the money.

This project fixes all three.

What we'll build

CapabilityWhat it does for us
Capture the moment interest showsThe instant a visitor types a valid phone/number — before they even pay — we save the lead. Plus a gentle pop-up for engaged visitors who haven't shared contact yet.
Call hot leads in 2–5 minutesA high-intent lead is handed to Superfone (our calling + WhatsApp agent) within seconds, so the team can reach them while they're still warm — with an alert if that ever slips past the deadline.

How it works, simply

Someone clicks our ad → lands on the site (we remember which campaign brought them) → shows interest → we capture their contact → if they're hot, Superfone calls/WhatsApps them in 2–5 minutes → whether or not they buy, the campaign gets credit for what happened. WhatsApp is offered after we've captured their details, so we never trade a real lead for a chat that might not happen.

What we need you to approve

1Superfone as our CRM + calling/WhatsApp agent

All calling and messaging happens in Superfone; we simply hand leads to it, fast. Approve Superfone as the tool?

2Sharing lead data with Superfone — the compliance point that needs legal ⚠️

To call a lead, Superfone needs their contact. Under India's data-protection law (DPDPA) that's sharing personal data with an outside party — and it's stricter for children (under-18), who need verifiable parental consent.

The honest gap: today our forms do not ask whether the person is a parent or the student — so we can't yet promise "parent contact only." We must fix that before sharing anything. Proposed handling:

  • Ask on the form — reusing what we already have. Our KAS form already carries a "parent / student" choice + a required consent tick + WhatsApp opt-in. We reuse that exact pattern on every lead form (school streams framed "a parent's number"). A lead only counts once consent is ticked.
  • Use the stream — adult streams (IAS/KAS/Corporate/Career) are adults; school streams (Class 5–10, NEET, JEE) are minors → the contact should be the parent.
  • We send only: the (parent/adult) contact + campaign & interest tags. Never a child's scores, DOB, or scorecard — none of that even exists at lead stage; it's gated behind guardian consent at onboarding.
  • Consent: contact given on the form + a logged WhatsApp opt-in. Agreement: a Data-Processing Agreement (DPA) with Superfone.

Key point: a lead is *pre-purchase* — consent to nurture/call is taken on the lead form itself (there is no later step to lean on). So for school streams we pass a lead to Superfone only when the parent gave the contact (form framed "a parent's number" + a one-tap "I'm the parent" confirmation); a lead without that signal is nurtured by message only, never cold-called. Legal to confirm this lead-form consent is sufficient for minors, and authorise a DPA with Superfone.
