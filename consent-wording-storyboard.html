Parent consent screen — wording, before & after

The screen a parent sees before their child's Success Scan. Same one tick, same one button — the words get clearer and honest to the law.

For sign-off · draft · not live to children until approved

Why change it

Today the parent ticks a single line that bundles two different things — “agree to the Terms” (a contract) and “approve for my child” (data consent) — and it says almost nothing about what they're approving. The law wants the child-data consent to stand on its own and to actually state what it covers. So we split the tick and give a lean, honest consent: the statement, one plain reassurance line, and a link to the full notice. The parent still does one tick and one button — the flow doesn't get longer.
