Children's privacy & consent — revised policy + what we need from you

Bringing the parent-buys-for-child flow in line with India's data-protection law (DPDPA 2023), in plain language.

Why this update (in one minute)

We're switching on the flow where a parent buys and enrols their school-age child. For a child's data, India's DPDPA 2023 asks for two things:

  • a parent's clear, specific consent before we use the child's data, and
  • a plain notice telling the parent what data we use, how to withdraw, and how to raise a complaint.

Our current privacy page pre-dates this law and was missing those pieces — and it even had a line implying a child's data could feed ads/tracking, which the law does not allow for children. This update fixes all of it. Nothing goes live to children until you sign off.

The parent consent — exactly what a parent ticks

Shown on screen before the child's Success Scan begins (plain English):

I am my child's parent or guardian, and I'm agreeing on their behalf.

I allow iRisePro to use my child's answers in the Success Scan to work out their abilities and create their personal report and suggestions.

I understand that:

  • This is my choice — I don't have to agree.
  • I can change my mind and withdraw at any time; then iRisePro stops using my child's data for this.
  • Changing my mind won't be held against my child, and won't affect anything already done.
  • iRisePro will never use my child's data for ads, tracking, or profiling.

Privacy Policy — new “Children's Data” section

Added to the Privacy Policy the consent links to (plain English):

What we collect, and why. Your child's answers in the Success Scan and the ability scores we work out from them, plus the details you give us (child's name, your name and contact). We use these only to score your child's abilities and create their report.

What we'll never do. We will never use your child's data for advertising, tracking, or profiling.

Your choice, and changing your mind. Consent is your choice. You can withdraw any time by emailing privacy@irisepro.in (subject: “Withdraw consent”); we then stop using your child's data for this. Anything already done isn't affected, and it's never held against your child.

Your rights & how to reach us. You can ask us to show, correct, or delete your child's data — contact our Grievance Officer. If we don't resolve it, you can complain to the Data Protection Board of India.

Also fixed in the Privacy Policy

  • Removed any suggestion that a child's data is used for ads or tracking (it isn't) — the marketing/analytics lines now clearly exclude anyone under 18.
  • Added — for everyone, not just children — how to withdraw consent, a Grievance Officer contact, and the right to complain to the Data Protection Board of India.
