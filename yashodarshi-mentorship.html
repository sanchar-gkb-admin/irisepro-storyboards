Non-blind by nature. Mentorship shows the real student (you're their mentor), the call link and the peer-mentor tag. It never touches the blind evaluation queue — the only crossing is the one-way “take on as mentee” bridge below.

Why two surfaces, not one. They run on different clocks and different rules:
Evaluation per-task · blind · SLA-paced (12h / 72h) · auto-allocated · you never pick students.
Mentorship a long-term ~1-year 1:1 relationship · named (not blind) · you choose to take a student on · call link + peer-mentor tag.
The bridge: evaluation naturally leads into mentorship — a one-way “take on as mentee” step carries a just-evaluated student across, at which point identity is (rightly) revealed because it's now a named, year-long relationship.
