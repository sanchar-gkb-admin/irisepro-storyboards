The purpose of benchmarking is not to label or compare students, but to scientifically identify strengths, growth opportunities and the pathway towards higher academic achievement and long-term success. For example, if a student scores 78% in Concentration while the Developmental Benchmark is 82%, the ASLI Champion Benchmark is 94% and the Competitive Exam Success Benchmark is 98%, it indicates that the student is progressing well but still has room to strengthen this ability to reach ASLI Champion-level and future competitive-exam-success levels. These benchmarks are not intended to compare or label students. Rather, they serve as scientific reference points that help students, parents and educators understand the student's current position, identify growth opportunities and build a clear pathway towards higher academic achievement and future success.
